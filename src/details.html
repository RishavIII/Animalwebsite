African Elephant

Overview

The African elephant (Loxodonta africana) is the largest land mammal on Earth, known for its remarkable intelligence, strong social bonds, and iconic tusks.

Habitat and Distribution

African elephants inhabit savannas, forests, deserts, and marshes across sub-Saharan Africa. They migrate seasonally in search of food and water.

Diet and Behavior

They are herbivores, consuming up to 300 pounds of vegetation per day, including grasses, leaves, bark, and fruit. Elephants live in matriarchal herds and communicate through infrasound.

Conservation Status

Classified as vulnerable by the IUCN, African elephants face threats from habitat loss and poaching for ivory. Conservation efforts focus on anti-poaching and habitat preservation.
